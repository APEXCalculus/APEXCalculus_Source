{$\ds\cosh^2x = \frac{\cosh2x+1}{2}$}
{\hfill$\begin{aligned}[t]
		\cosh^2x &= \left(\frac{e^x+e^{-x}}{2}\right)^2  \\
											&= \frac{e^{2x} + 2 + e^{-2x}}{4} \\
											&= \frac12\frac{(e^{2x}  + e^{-2x})+2}{2}\\
											&= \frac12\left(\frac{e^{2x}  + e^{-2x}}{2}+1\right)\\
											&= \frac{\cosh2x+1}{2}.			
\end{aligned}$\hfill\null}
